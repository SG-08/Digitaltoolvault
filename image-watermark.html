Why Watermark Your Images?

A watermark is a visible or subtle overlay on an image that identifies its owner or source. Watermarking is the simplest way to protect your visual content from being used without permission — and to ensure credit reaches you even if the image is shared without attribution.

Who Needs Image Watermarking?

  • Photographers — protect portfolio images from being used without licensing
  • Content creators — brand social media images with your handle or logo
  • E-commerce sellers — prevent product photos from being stolen by competitors
  • Businesses — brand all visual content before sharing online
  • Educators — mark worksheets, presentations and documents with institution name
  • Real estate agents — watermark property listing photos

Watermark Best Practices

FactorBest Practice
PositionBottom-right or centre — most visible but least disruptive
Opacity40–70% — visible but not overpowering the main image
Font sizeLarge enough to be readable but not dominating
Text contentWebsite URL, @handle, brand name, or copyright notice
ColourWhite or black with partial opacity works on most image backgrounds
💡 For professional photography, place a semi-transparent watermark diagonally across the centre of the image — this prevents cropping-based removal while keeping the subject visible.

Frequently Asked Questions
