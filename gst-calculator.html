What is GST?

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is a unified indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services across India. It replaced multiple indirect taxes including VAT, service tax, and excise duty. Introduced in July 2017, GST is structured into four main slabs based on the type of goods or services.

GST Rate Slabs in India

GST Rate Category / Examples
0%Essential food items, fresh vegetables, milk, books, education
5%Packaged food, transport services, household necessities
12%Processed food, business class air travel, computers
18%Most services, electronics, restaurants, telecom, banking
28%Luxury goods, automobiles, tobacco, air conditioners, cement

GST Calculation Formula

Adding GST (Exclusive to Inclusive)

GST Amount = Original Amount × (GST Rate ÷ 100)
Post-GST Price = Original Amount + GST Amount

Removing GST (Inclusive to Exclusive)

Pre-GST Price = GST-Inclusive Price ÷ (1 + GST Rate ÷ 100)
GST Amount = GST-Inclusive Price − Pre-GST Price

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is the GST rate on services?
Most services in India attract 18% GST. However, healthcare and education are exempt, while some services fall under 5% or 12%.

How do I calculate 18% GST on ₹10,000?
GST Amount = ₹10,000 × 18% = ₹1,800. Post-GST Price = ₹11,800. Use the calculator above to verify instantly.

What is the difference between inclusive and exclusive GST?
Exclusive GST means the displayed price does not include tax — GST is added on top. Inclusive GST means tax is already built into the price.
