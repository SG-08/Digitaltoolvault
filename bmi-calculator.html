What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a numerical value calculated from a person's weight and height. It provides a simple, widely-used indicator of whether a person has a healthy body weight relative to their height. BMI is used by doctors, nutritionists and health organisations worldwide as a quick screening tool for weight-related health risks.

BMI Formula

BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height² (m²)

Example: Weight = 70 kg, Height = 1.75 m → BMI = 70 ÷ (1.75 × 1.75) = 22.9

BMI for Indian Adults

The WHO BMI thresholds above are designed for Western populations. Research shows that Indians and other South Asians have a higher risk of metabolic diseases at lower BMI values. The Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) recommends using modified cut-offs for Indian adults:

BMI RangeCategory (Indian Adults)
Below 18.0Underweight
18.0 – 22.9Normal Weight
23.0 – 24.9Overweight
25.0 and aboveObese
⚠️ BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnosis. It does not account for muscle mass, bone density, body fat distribution or age. Consult a doctor for a complete health assessment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is BMI accurate for athletes?No. Muscle is denser than fat, so athletes with high muscle mass often have a high BMI despite having low body fat. BMI can misclassify fit, muscular individuals as overweight or obese.
What is a healthy BMI for women vs men?The BMI scale itself does not differ by gender. However, women naturally carry more body fat than men at the same BMI. A BMI of 22 is considered equally healthy for both sexes under standard guidelines.
How do I reduce my BMI?BMI decreases when you lose body fat. The most effective approach combines a calorie-controlled diet, regular aerobic exercise (150+ minutes/week) and strength training. Consult a nutritionist for a personalised plan.
Can children use this BMI calculator?No. Adult BMI categories do not apply to children. For children aged 2–18, BMI is calculated the same way but assessed against age- and sex-specific percentile charts (CDC or WHO growth charts).
What is the BMI of a 70 kg person who is 5'7" tall?5'7" = 1.70 m. BMI = 70 ÷ (1.70 × 1.70) = 70 ÷ 2.89 ≈ 24.2 — which falls in the Normal/Healthy range.
