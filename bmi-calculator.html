BMI Formula

BMI is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by the square of your height in meters:

BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height² (m²)

Example Calculation

If your weight is 70 kg and height is 175 cm (1.75 m):
BMI = 70 ÷ (1.75 × 1.75) = 70 ÷ 3.0625 = 22.86 (Normal)

Limitations of BMI

While BMI is a useful screening tool, it does have limitations. It does not directly measure body fat and may not accurately reflect health in all individuals. The following groups may get misleading BMI readings:

  • Athletes and bodybuilders — High muscle mass can result in a high BMI despite low body fat
  • Elderly individuals — May have normal BMI but high body fat due to muscle loss
  • Children and teenagers — BMI needs to be interpreted using age and gender-specific charts
  • Pregnant women — BMI is not an appropriate measure during pregnancy

Always consult a healthcare professional for a complete health assessment.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is BMI?
BMI (Body Mass Index) is a measure of body fat based on your height and weight. It helps determine whether you have a healthy body weight relative to your height.

Is BMI accurate?
BMI provides a general estimate of healthy weight. It may not fully account for muscle mass, bone density, or body composition, so it should be used alongside other health assessments.

What is a healthy BMI for Indians?
For Indian adults, a BMI between 18.5 and 22.9 is considered normal. A BMI of 23 or above may indicate increased health risk according to Indian health guidelines.

How often should I check my BMI?
Checking your BMI every 3 to 6 months is generally sufficient for tracking weight management progress. More frequent checks are not necessary unless advised by a doctor.
