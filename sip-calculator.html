// Overview

WHAT IS THE SIP CALCULATOR?

A SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) Calculator helps you estimate the future value of your regular monthly investments in a mutual fund. SIP is one of the most popular and disciplined ways to invest in India, allowing you to invest a fixed amount every month regardless of market conditions.

The SIP calculator uses compound interest mathematics to show you how your money grows over time through the power of compounding. Even small monthly amounts, invested consistently over long periods, can grow into substantial wealth thanks to compounding returns.

India's mutual fund industry (tracked by AMFI) has seen explosive growth in SIP investments, with over 8 crore active SIP accounts. Understanding how SIP returns work is essential for anyone building long-term financial goals like retirement, children's education, or buying a home.

// Formula

THE FORMULA EXPLAINED

SIP maturity value is calculated using the Future Value of an Annuity formula:

FV = P × [(1+r)^n – 1] / r × (1+r)

Where P = Monthly investment, r = Monthly interest rate (Annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), n = Number of months.

Example: ₹5,000/month for 10 years at 12% p.a. → FV ≈ ₹11.6 lakhs invested, maturity ≈ ₹23.2 lakhs — nearly doubling your money.

// Pro Tips

TIPS & BEST PRACTICES

  • Start early: A 25-year-old investing ₹5,000/month for 35 years will accumulate significantly more than a 35-year-old investing ₹10,000/month for 25 years — because time beats amount.
  • Step-up your SIP: Increase your SIP amount by 10–15% every year as your income grows.
  • Stay invested during downturns: SIP benefits from rupee cost averaging — you buy more units when markets fall.
  • Use 12% for equity, 7% for debt: These are reasonable long-term return assumptions for Indian mutual funds.
  • Keep a 3-month emergency fund before starting a SIP so you never have to redeem early.

// FAQ

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is SIP?
SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is a method of investing a fixed amount in a mutual fund at regular intervals (usually monthly). It promotes disciplined investing and benefits from rupee cost averaging and the power of compounding.
Is SIP calculator result guaranteed?
No. The SIP calculator gives estimated returns based on an assumed fixed rate of return. Actual mutual fund returns vary depending on market conditions, fund performance, and the type of fund. Use this as a planning tool, not a guarantee.
What return rate should I use for SIP calculation?
For equity mutual funds (large-cap), 10–12% p.a. is a reasonable long-term assumption. For mid/small-cap funds, 12–15%. For debt funds, 6–8%. For ELSS (tax-saving), 10–12%.
What is the minimum SIP amount in India?
Most mutual funds in India allow SIPs starting from ₹100–₹500 per month. Some funds like Axis, Mirae Asset, and Parag Parikh accept ₹500 minimums.
Is SIP good for long-term investment?
Yes, SIP is widely considered one of the best long-term wealth-building strategies for retail investors in India. The combination of disciplined investing, rupee cost averaging, and compound growth makes it powerful over 10+ year periods.
Can I stop a SIP anytime?
Yes. Most mutual funds allow you to pause or stop your SIP without any penalty, giving you flexibility unlike fixed deposits or insurance endowment plans.
