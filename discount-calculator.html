// Overview

WHAT IS A DISCOUNT CALCULATOR?

A Discount Calculator finds the final price of a product after a percentage discount is applied. Whether shopping on Amazon, Flipkart, Myntra, or at a local sale, this tool tells you the exact amount you pay and how much you save — without mental arithmetic on irregular MRP prices like Rs 1,899 or Rs 12,490.

Discounts are everywhere in India: Amazon Great Indian Festival, Flipkart Big Billion Days, end-of-season sales, and daily promotional pricing. Our calculator handles any percentage on any amount in milliseconds.

Understanding true discount value also matters for retail pricing — businesses need to set sale prices that remain profitable, and consumers need to verify that advertised savings are genuine rather than based on inflated original prices.

// Smart Shopping

SMART SHOPPING TIPS

  • Always verify the MRP is the actual MRP and not an inflated price used to exaggerate the discount
  • For stacked discounts (20% off + extra 10%): apply sequentially — 20% off Rs 1,000 = Rs 800, then 10% off Rs 800 = Rs 720 (not Rs 700 as 30% direct)
  • Compare final prices across Amazon, Flipkart, and Meesho before buying
  • For bulk offers, calculate per-unit cost to verify the deal is genuine

// FAQ

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How do I calculate 25% off Rs 2,000?
Sale price = 2000 x 0.75 = Rs 1,500. Savings = Rs 500.
What is 40% off Rs 1,999?
Savings = Rs 799.60. Sale price = Rs 1,199.40.
How do I find original price from a discounted price?
Original = Discounted Price / (1 - Discount%/100). Paid Rs 1,400 after 30% off: Rs 1,400 / 0.70 = Rs 2,000.
Are stacked discounts additive?
No. 20% + 10% is not 30%. Apply them sequentially.
