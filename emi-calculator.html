What is EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It is the fixed amount a borrower pays to a lender every month until the loan is fully repaid. Each EMI consists of two parts: the principal repayment (the original loan amount) and the interest charge (the cost of borrowing). In the early months of a loan, a larger portion of the EMI goes toward interest. Over time, as the outstanding principal reduces, more of each payment goes toward the principal.

EMI Formula

EMI = P × r × (1 + r)ⁿ ÷ [(1 + r)ⁿ – 1]

Where:

  • P = Principal loan amount (₹)
  • r = Monthly interest rate = Annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100
  • n = Loan tenure in months

Example: Loan of ₹10,00,000 at 9% per annum for 5 years (60 months):
r = 9 ÷ 12 ÷ 100 = 0.0075 | EMI ≈ ₹20,758 | Total Interest ≈ ₹2,45,480

Tips to Reduce Your EMI

  • Larger down payment — reduces principal, lowering EMI directly
  • Longer tenure — spreads the loan over more months, reducing each EMI (but increases total interest)
  • Better credit score — score above 750 often qualifies for lower interest rates
  • Compare lenders — even a 0.5% difference in rate can save thousands over 20 years
  • Part-prepayment — paying extra principal mid-loan reduces outstanding balance and EMI
💡 Pro Tip: A lower EMI does not always mean a better deal. A longer tenure reduces monthly burden but significantly increases total interest paid. Use this calculator to compare different tenure options before deciding.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between flat rate and reducing balance EMI?In a flat rate EMI, interest is calculated on the original principal throughout the tenure. In reducing balance (used by most Indian banks), interest is calculated on the outstanding balance each month — so you pay less interest overall. This calculator uses the reducing balance method.
Can I calculate EMI for a home loan with a floating rate?This calculator uses a fixed rate. For floating rate loans, calculate EMI at the current rate and recalculate whenever your bank revises the rate.
What happens if I miss an EMI payment?Missing an EMI results in a late payment penalty (usually 1–2% per month on overdue amount), a negative impact on your CIBIL score and possible legal action if multiple EMIs are missed.
Is this EMI calculator accurate?Yes, it uses the standard reducing balance formula used by all major Indian banks and RBI-regulated NBFCs. Results may vary slightly due to rounding but are within ₹1–2 of actual bank calculations.
Can I use this for car loan, home loan and personal loan?Yes. This calculator works for all loan types — home loan, car loan, personal loan, education loan, gold loan — as long as you enter the correct principal, rate and tenure.
