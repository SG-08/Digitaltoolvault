What is a Percentage?

A percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. The word comes from the Latin per centum, meaning "per hundred." Percentages are used everywhere — exam scores, tax rates, discounts, interest rates and data analysis.

The 5 Calculations This Tool Covers

1. What is X% of Y?

Result = (X ÷ 100) × Y  |  Example: 20% of 500 = 100

Use this for GST on a price, salary hike value, tip amount or discount amount.

2. X is What Percentage of Y?

Percentage = (X ÷ Y) × 100  |  Example: 75 of 300 = 25%

Use this to find exam percentage, market share or budget utilisation.

3. Percentage Change

Change% = ((New − Old) ÷ Old) × 100  |  Example: 800→1000 = +25%

Use this for price changes, salary hikes, stock returns or growth rates.

4. Discount Calculator

Sale Price = Original × (1 − Discount% ÷ 100)  |  Example: ₹1999 at 30% off = ₹1399.30

Instantly find what you actually pay after a discount during sales on Amazon, Flipkart or any store.

5. Exam Marks Percentage

Percentage = (Marks Obtained ÷ Total Marks) × 100  |  Example: 450/600 = 75%

Use for board exams, semester results, competitive tests or any scored assessment.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate percentage of a number?Multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100. Example: 20% of 500 = (500 × 20) ÷ 100 = 100. Or multiply by the decimal: 500 × 0.20 = 100.
How do I calculate percentage increase?Percentage increase = ((New Value − Old Value) ÷ Old Value) × 100. Salary from ₹40,000 to ₹48,000 = ((48000−40000) ÷ 40000) × 100 = 20%.
How do I calculate my exam percentage?Divide marks obtained by total marks and multiply by 100. Example: 368 out of 500 = (368 ÷ 500) × 100 = 73.6%.
How do I find the original price before discount?Original Price = Sale Price ÷ (1 − Discount% ÷ 100). Example: Paid ₹700 after 30% off → original = 700 ÷ 0.70 = ₹1,000.
What is 1% of 1 crore?1% of 1,00,00,000 = ₹1,00,000 (one lakh).
