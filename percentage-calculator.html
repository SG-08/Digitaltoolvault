What is a Percentage?

A percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. The word comes from the Latin per centum, meaning "per hundred." Percentages are used everywhere — from exam scores and tax rates to discounts, interest rates and data analysis. Understanding percentages is one of the most practical everyday maths skills.

The 5 Percentage Calculations This Tool Covers

1. What is X% of Y? (Percentage Of)

Result = (X ÷ 100) × Y
Example: 20% of 500 = (20 ÷ 100) × 500 = 100

Use this for: calculating GST on a price, finding a tip amount, computing a salary hike value, or finding a discount amount.

2. X is What Percentage of Y?

Percentage = (X ÷ Y) × 100
Example: 75 is what % of 300? = (75 ÷ 300) × 100 = 25%

Use this for: finding your exam percentage, calculating market share, finding what portion of a budget is spent.

3. Percentage Change (Increase or Decrease)

Change% = ((New Value − Old Value) ÷ Old Value) × 100
Example: Price goes from ₹800 to ₹1,000 → Change = ((1000−800) ÷ 800) × 100 = +25%

Use this for: comparing prices over time, tracking stock performance, calculating salary growth.

4. Discount Calculator

Sale Price = Original Price × (1 − Discount% ÷ 100)
Savings = Original Price − Sale Price
Example: ₹1,999 at 30% off → ₹1,999 × 0.70 = ₹1,399.30

Use this for: shopping on Amazon, Flipkart or during sale seasons — instantly find the actual price after discount.

5. Exam Marks Percentage

Percentage = (Marks Obtained ÷ Total Marks) × 100
Example: 450 out of 600 = (450 ÷ 600) × 100 = 75%

Use this for: calculating your percentage in board exams, semester results, entrance tests or any scored assessment.

Percentage in Daily Life — India Specific

  • GST — 5%, 12%, 18%, 28% on goods and services — calculated as percentage of base price
  • Bank interest — FD, savings account and loan rates are expressed as annual percentage
  • TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) — 10% on salary above threshold, 1% on property purchases
  • Exam results — CBSE, JEE, NEET, UPSC all express results as percentages or percentile
  • Stock market — daily gains, annual returns and index movements all use percentage change
  • Discount shopping — every sale tag on Flipkart, Amazon and Myntra shows percentage off
  • Salary negotiations — hike percentages, variable pay percentages, provident fund (12% of basic)
💡 Quick mental maths trick: To find 10% of any number, just move the decimal point one place left. So 10% of ₹3,500 = ₹350. For 5%, halve the 10% figure (₹175). For 20%, double the 10% figure (₹700).

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate percentage of a number?Multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100. Example: 20% of 500 = (500 × 20) ÷ 100 = 100. Or simply multiply by the decimal equivalent: 500 × 0.20 = 100.
How do I calculate percentage increase?Percentage increase = ((New Value − Old Value) ÷ Old Value) × 100. If your salary went from ₹40,000 to ₹48,000, the increase = ((48000−40000) ÷ 40000) × 100 = 20%.
How do I calculate my exam percentage?Divide marks obtained by total marks and multiply by 100. Example: 368 out of 500 = (368 ÷ 500) × 100 = 73.6%.
How do I find the original price after discount?Original Price = Sale Price ÷ (1 − Discount% ÷ 100). Example: If you paid ₹700 after a 30% discount, original = 700 ÷ 0.70 = ₹1,000.
What is 1% of 1 crore?1% of 1,00,00,000 = (1 ÷ 100) × 1,00,00,000 = ₹1,00,000 (one lakh).
