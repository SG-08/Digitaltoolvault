Why Convert Images to PDF?

PDF (Portable Document Format) is the universal standard for sharing documents. Converting images to PDF ensures your content is viewable on any device without image viewer software, maintains original quality and layout, and is widely accepted for official submissions — government forms, university applications, HR onboarding and more.

Common Use Cases

  • Scanned documents — photos of ID cards, bills, certificates → single PDF for email or upload
  • University admissions — combine multiple document photos into one PDF submission
  • Job applications — merge photo ID, degree certificate, experience letter into one PDF
  • E-commerce invoices — convert product photo + invoice image to a professional PDF
  • Presentations — save slide screenshots as a PDF for easy sharing
  • Photo albums — create a PDF photo book from multiple images

Image to PDF Tips

TipWhy it Matters
Use high-res images (300 DPI+)Prevents blurry or pixelated PDF output
Use consistent image orientationAvoids mixed portrait/landscape pages in PDF
Order images before convertingPDF pages follow the order you add images
Compress images firstReduces final PDF file size significantly
💡 For government document submissions (passport, VISA, bank KYC), use JPG images scanned at 200–300 DPI. The resulting PDF is typically accepted by all Indian government portals including DigiLocker and UIDAI.
