Simple Interest Formula

SI = (P × R × T) ÷ 100  |  Total Amount = P + SI

Where P = Principal, R = Annual Rate (%), T = Time (Years)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is simple interest?Simple interest is calculated only on the original principal amount, not on accumulated interest. Formula: SI = (P × R × T) / 100.
When is simple interest used in India?Simple interest is used in certain car loans, gold loans, short-term personal loans and government savings schemes like NSC (National Savings Certificate).
Is simple interest better for borrowers?Yes. Since interest is charged only on the original principal, total interest paid under simple interest is always less than compound interest for the same rate and duration.
