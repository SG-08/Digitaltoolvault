How Currency Exchange Rates Work

A currency exchange rate is the price of one currency expressed in terms of another. Exchange rates fluctuate constantly based on supply and demand in the global foreign exchange (forex) market — the world's largest financial market, with over $7 trillion traded daily. Rates are influenced by inflation, interest rates, political stability, economic performance and market speculation.

Types of Exchange Rates

  • Spot Rate — the current market rate for immediate currency exchange
  • Forward Rate — a rate agreed today for a transaction at a future date (used in forex hedging)
  • Interbank Rate — the rate banks charge each other (the "mid-market" rate you see online)
  • Retail Rate — what banks/exchange bureaus charge individuals — includes a spread/commission
💡 When exchanging currency for travel, compare the "total cost" including all fees, not just the headline rate. Online forex services (like Wise or Niyo) often offer better rates than airport currency counters.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to convert currency?Currency markets are most liquid during business hours in major financial centres (London, New York, Tokyo). The INR/USD rate is most actively traded during overlap hours between Indian and US market hours (6:30–8:30 PM IST).
What is TT Rate (Telegraphic Transfer Rate)?TT rate is the exchange rate banks use for international wire transfers. It differs slightly from the mid-market rate — banks add a margin (usually 0.5–2%) as their fee.
Why does the USD/INR rate change daily?The INR is a managed float currency — the RBI intervenes in forex markets to prevent excessive volatility, but the rate still moves daily based on trade flows, FII investments, oil prices and global USD strength.
